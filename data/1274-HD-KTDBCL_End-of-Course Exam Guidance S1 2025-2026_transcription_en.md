# GUIDANCE ON THE IMPLEMENTATION OF END-OF-COURSE EXAMINATIONS FOR THE FIRST SEMESTER OF THE 2025-2026 ACADEMIC YEAR

Pursuant to Official Dispatch No. 840/ĐHVN-ĐT dated July 30, 2025, on the development of the academic schedule for the 2025-2026 academic year at Vietnam Japan University; Pursuant to Decision No. 1132/QĐ-ĐHVN dated October 1, 2025, on the issuance of regulations concerning examination activities at Vietnam Japan University; To improve the quality of education and ensure the implementation of end-of-course examinations in accordance with the academic schedule, Vietnam Japan University hereby provides the following guidance to all units, faculty, staff, and students regarding the implementation of grading and end-of-course examinations for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year.

## 1. Implementation Principles

  - To ensure integrity, accuracy, fairness, and transparency in the administration of end-of-course examinations.
  - To ensure the rights of learners in the evaluation of end-of-course examination results.
  - To ensure that the requirements for assessing the achievement of the course's learning outcomes are met.
  - To ensure compliance with the progress, plans, and academic schedules of Vietnam Japan University and external institutions (including VNU Center for Physical Education and Sports, VNU Institute for Digital Education and Examination, and VNU University of Languages and International Studies) for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year.

### 2.2. Examination Implementation Process

The implementation of end-of-course examinations for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year includes developing the examination plan and announcing the schedule, creating questions and grading, assigning proctors, entering and announcing grades, and handling re-grading applications. The specific process is detailed in Appendix 2.

### 2.3. Requirements for Creating Examination Questions and Answer Keys

  - End-of-course examination questions must be able to assess the learning outcomes / graduate standards (LOs) of the course.
  - Examination questions must not be identical to those from previous semester examinations, quizzes, practice problems, or review questions that have been previously disclosed to learners. This includes courses where examination papers are required to be collected.
  - For courses offered to multiple classes, a common examination paper shall be created, with prior agreement on the requirements and basic content of the examination among all responsible faculty members and the Program Director/Dean (approval from the Rector's Office is required for special cases).
  - For courses with an existing question bank, the Head of the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance shall compile the examination questions based on the approved question bank.
  - For examinations in the form of reports, major assignments, or essays, the submission deadline and method shall be proposed by the responsible faculty member and approved through the Program Director/Dean, in accordance with the University's academic and examination plan. The responsible faculty member is accountable for executing the plan, collecting reports, grading, submitting questions, providing grading rubrics, submitting reports/essays, and submitting the end-of-course examination grade sheet to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance on time. Learners must follow the faculty's instructions precisely.
  - For oral examinations: Each question must have a clear scoring rubric, ensuring completeness, accuracy, comprehensiveness, applicability, uniform difficulty among questions, and consistency with the number of credits and the duration of the end-of-course examination.
  - Duration of end-of-course examinations:
  - Essay or combined essay and multiple-choice format: 30 minutes per credit, but not exceeding 120 minutes for courses of 4 credits or more.
  - Multiple-choice format: 20 minutes per credit, but not exceeding 90 minutes for courses of 4 credits or more.
  - Oral examination: 15 minutes for preparation; approximately 7-10 minutes for presenting the answer and addressing additional questions from the faculty. For courses requiring more than 15 minutes of preparation time, this must be specified in the detailed syllabus.
  - Examination questions must be accompanied by an answer key with a scoring rubric and a clear grading scale. The grading scale is a 10-point scale, rounded to one decimal place. For multiple-choice or foreign language examinations, grading may be done on a different scale and then converted to the 10-point scale (refer to the templates in Appendices 3 and 4).
  - The examination questions and the answer key must be placed in separate envelopes, sealed, and have a seal affixed across the flap. Each envelope must clearly state the same information as the top of the examination paper, along with the name and phone number of the creator. The creator of the answer key must sign and write their full name on the answer key (according to the attached template). If necessary, the University will publish the answer key to allow learners to review their own answer scripts.
  - Number of question versions per examination session:
  - Essay, combined essay and multiple-choice: A minimum of 2 different versions of questions per examination session.
  - Multiple-choice: A minimum of 2 different question codes for paper-based tests and a minimum of 4 different question codes for computer-based tests.
  - Oral examination: Questions shall be created in sets (minimum of 10 questions), and learners will draw a question to answer during the examination session.
  - Format of end-of-course examination questions:
  - Examination questions must be typed or clearly handwritten in black or dark blue ink on one side of A4 paper, with a margin of at least 2 cm from the edge of the paper.
  - The top of the examination paper must clearly state the course name, course code, number of credits, question version number, and examination duration.
  - The bottom of the examination paper must clearly state if learners are permitted to use materials.
  - For multiple-choice examinations, the multiple-choice answer sheet template must be attached to the examination paper (see Appendix 3c).

### 2.4. Requirements for Grading

  - Assignment of graders: The Dean/Program Director is responsible for assigning faculty members to grade the end-of-course examination answer scripts, ensuring that each course is handled by at least two faculty members. The grading process must be conducted with rigor, objectivity, accuracy, and fairness.
  - Grading and Grade Submission Process
  - Grading on paper (essay, combined essay and multiple-choice, and multiple-choice answer sheets).
  - The Department of Examination & Quality Assurance is responsible for preparing a room for faculty to grade and for managing the grading progress.
  - Starting the day after the course examination date, faculty members responsible for grading can collect the answer scripts from the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance. Graders must submit the graded mark sheets and answer scripts to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance no later than two weeks (10 working days) after the course examination date.
  - Grading of end-of-course examination answer scripts must follow the official answer key and scoring rubric submitted to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance. Faculty members shall grade directly on the answer scripts, using a 10-point scale with one decimal place. The answer scripts must be signed by two grading faculty members. If errors are discovered, faculty members shall request a grade correction, clearly stating the reason for the correction.
  - Grading in the computer lab
  - For courses graded directly on the computer: After the examination time ends, learners must stop their work and remain seated while the proctor verifies the results and records the examination score displayed on the computer screen onto the mark sheet. Learners must re-verify the result on the mark sheet before signing the examinee list. The proctor is responsible for the accuracy of the examination scores recorded on the mark sheet. The mark sheet shall be made in triplicate, with the original kept at the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance, one copy for the faculty, and one copy for the Academic Affairs & Student Activities.
  - For courses that cannot be graded directly in the examination room: The proctor is responsible for saving the learners' answer scripts to a CD or USB immediately after each examination session ends. Immediately after the examination, the proctor shall hand over the examinee list and the CD or USB containing the learners' answer scripts to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance. The Department of Examination & Quality Assurance shall hand over the CD or USB containing the learners' answer scripts to the staff responsible for grading, in accordance with regulations.
  - Grading of end-of-course examination answer scripts must follow the official answer key and scoring rubric submitted to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance. Faculty members shall record the scores on a grading sheet and return the CD or USB containing the answer scripts, the grading sheet, and the mark sheet to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance no later than two weeks (10 working days) after the course examination date.
  - Grading of presentations and reports: Grading of end-of-course examination answer scripts must follow the official answer key and scoring rubric submitted to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance. Faculty members shall record the scores on a grading sheet and submit the grading sheet, answer scripts, and mark sheet to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance no later than two weeks (10 working days) after the course examination date.
  - Grading of oral examinations: Grading of end-of-course examination answer scripts must follow the official answer key and scoring rubric submitted to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance. For oral examination formats without audio recordings of the learners' answers, the examination scores must be announced to the learners immediately after the examination and submitted to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance no later than one working day from the end-of-course examination date.

### 2.5. Processing of Re-grading Results

  - Immediately upon receiving feedback and re-grading requests from learners, the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance shall retrieve the answer scripts subject to re-grading and notify the academic unit. The Dean/Program Director shall appoint graders to conduct the re-grading within a maximum of 7 working days. For general education courses, the process shall follow the regulations of Vietnam National University, Hanoi.
  - The processing of re-grading results shall be conducted in the same manner as the initial grading.
  - If the re-graded score differs from the initial (published) score by 0.5 to 1.0 point, the score shall be corrected immediately.
  - If the re-graded score differs from the initial score by more than 1.0 point, the Dean/Program Director shall appoint a staff member different from the initial grader to facilitate a direct dialogue between the initial grader and the second grader to agree on the re-graded score, finalize the score, and create a record of the meeting.
  - If, during the grade processing, any involved party detects signs of intentional misconduct, they shall report it to the Department of Inspection and Legal Affairs for resolution.
  - The re-grading results shall be processed by the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance in accordance with regulations and forwarded to the Academic Affairs & Student Activities for notification to learners no later than 3 working days after the end of the re-grading period.

## 3. Responsibilities of Units and Individuals

### 3.1. Department of Examination & Quality Assurance

  - Consolidate information regarding the format of end-of-course examinations from each academic coordinating unit.
  - Create the schedule for end-of-course examinations.
  - Create the list of examinees and arrange examination rooms at the designated time.
  - Notify all relevant parties of the examination schedule at the designated time.
  - Create a list for mobilizing proctors (CBCT), send it to the relevant units, and urge them to send the list of assigned proctors by the specified deadline.
  - Receive and store grading rubrics, examples of graded component assessment scripts, final grade sheets, and end-of-course examination answer scripts. The storage method and duration shall comply with the current guidance and regulations of the Ministry of Education and Training and Vietnam National University, Hanoi.
  - Receive examination questions and answer keys from faculty members; manage the question bank, end-of-course examination questions (printed copies), and computer data files.
  - Shuffle, draw, print, and duplicate examination questions in accordance with the University's examination processes, schedules, and regulations, and adhere to confidentiality requirements as prescribed by law.
  - Prepare examination rooms (registering examination rooms, waiting rooms; having security arrange desks and chairs according to the number of examinees on the list).
  - Prepare grading rooms; hand over examination questions, answer sheets/multiple-choice answer sheets to proctors, and receive answer scripts. Hand over answer scripts, end-of-course examination mark sheets, and guidance/regulations on grading. Urge relevant units to grade, return answer scripts, and submit mark sheets in accordance with regulations.
  - Inform learners of the period and process for submitting re-grading applications. Be responsible for the re-grading of end-of-course examination answer scripts upon request from learners.

### 3.2. Academic Affairs & Student Activities

  - Supervise and urge faculty members to confirm the format of final examinations for courses coordinated by the Academic Affairs & Student Activities.
  - Supervise and urge faculties, programs, and faculty members to submit component assessment grade sheets for each course each semester.
  - Provide faculty members with accounts for the grade entry software. Handle issues related to grade entry software accounts. Notify faculty members of the timeline for updating component assessment scores and submitting grade sheets on the University's current grade entry software.
  - Receive and handle issues related to component assessment grade data that arise between students and faculty.
  - Receive end-of-course examination grade data from the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance. Update course evaluation grade data on the academic management software and the academic information portal for learners to access.

### 3.3. Other Functional Departments

  - Department of Planning & Finance: Coordinate with the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance to prepare the budget for examinations; provide the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance with a list of learners who have not paid their tuition fees.
  - Administration Office: Inspect and supervise the examination implementation process in accordance with the functions and authority of the Department of Inspection and Legal Affairs. Cooperate with relevant parties to advise, guide, and coordinate for the smooth implementation of tasks.
  - Units and individuals in charge of classrooms, practice rooms, and computer labs are responsible for the physical facilities of examinations that use the facilities under their management.
  - Leaders of functional departments are responsible for dispatching proctors upon request from the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance.

### 3.4. Deans/Program Directors, Co-Directors

  - Disseminate the regulations on examination work and the requirements of end-of-course examinations to all staff and learners.
  - Dispatch faculty members, researchers, or faculty assistants as proctors to participate in proctoring duties.
  - Appoint course instructors, question creators, and graders for courses directly managed by the faculty.

### 3.5. Faculty Members

  - Send the list of learners not eligible to take the examination to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance.
  - Submit examination questions, answer keys/grading rubrics, and end-of-course examination grades to the Department of Examination & Quality Assurance within the specified timeframe.
  - Enter end-of-course examination grades into the grade entry system.
  - Conduct re-grading as required by regulations (if applicable).

### 3.6. Proctors

  - Demonstrate a high sense of responsibility and fully understand the regulations on proctoring.
  - Accurately execute the end-of-course examination proctoring process as described in Appendix 5.

### 3.7. Undergraduate and Graduate Students

  - Pay tuition fees in full and on time.
  - Proactively study and prepare for examinations to achieve good results.
  - Comply with the responsibilities of examinees in the examination room as stipulated in Appendix 7.

The Rector's Office requests that all units proactively coordinate to implement the examinations and disseminate the above contents to all faculty, professional staff, and employees for execution.

# Appendix 7: Responsibilities of Examinees in the Examination Room

1. Students must be present at the examination venue at the designated time as per regulations. Students who are more than 15 minutes late after the examination questions have been distributed will not be permitted to take the exam.
2. Upon entering the examination room, students must sit at the seat designated by their examinee number.
3. Students may only bring permitted items into the examination room, such as writing instruments, rulers, and personal calculators without text composition or memory card functions.
4. Students are not permitted to bring into the examination room: draft paper, carbon paper, mobile phones, weapons, explosives, transmitting/receiving/communication/recording/video devices, or any equipment containing information that could be used for the examination. Smoking is prohibited in the examination area and examination room.
5. For courses where the use of materials is permitted in the examination room, students may bring materials as specified in the end-of-course examination questions, in accordance with the faculty's regulations.
6. During the examination, students must follow the instructions of the proctors. Students must maintain silence and a serious attitude throughout the examination.
7. Handling of violations shall be in accordance with the Undergraduate Academic Regulations 3626 (Article 35) and the Master's Program Academic Regulations 3636 (Article 46).

> Students are responsible for complying with the regulations on discipline in the examination room; violations will result in disciplinary action at the following levels:
> a) Reprimand: Applied to students who commit a one-time violation such as looking at another person's paper, exchanging papers, or talking during the examination. A student who receives a reprimand in a course will have 25% of their score for that examination deducted.
> b) Warning: Applied to students who commit one of the following violations:
>   - Having been reprimanded once but continuing to violate regulations during the examination for that course.
>   - Exchanging answer scripts or draft papers with others.
>   - Copying another person's paper. Papers concluded to be identical will be handled similarly. However, if the disciplined individual has sufficient evidence to prove they were the one being copied from, the head of the institution may consider reducing the penalty from a warning to a reprimand.
> A student who receives a warning in a course will have 50% of their score for that examination deducted.
> c) Suspension from the examination: Applied to students who commit one of the following violations:
>   - Having been warned once but continuing to violate regulations during the examination for that course.
>   - Being discovered with prohibited items after the examination questions have been distributed.
> A student who receives a suspension from the examination will be given a score of 0 for that course's examination and must leave the examination room immediately after the decision to suspend is made. The disciplinary actions mentioned above shall be implemented by the proctor, who will create a report, collect evidence, and clearly state the form of disciplinary action.
> d) Students who engage in examination impersonation or ask others to take an examination on their behalf will be disciplined with a one-year suspension for the first offense, and expulsion for the second offense.
