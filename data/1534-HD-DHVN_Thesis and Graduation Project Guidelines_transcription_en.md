# GUIDANCE

**On the organization of undergraduate theses and graduation projects for full-time students at Vietnam-Japan University, Vietnam National University, Hanoi**

*Pursuant to Decision No. 1186/QĐ-TTg dated July 21, 2014 of the Prime Minister on the establishment of Vietnam-Japan University under Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

*Pursuant to Decision No. 391/QĐ-TTg dated March 31, 2017 of the Prime Minister on the Regulations on organization and operation of Vietnam-Japan University under Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

*Pursuant to Decision No. 5115/QĐ-ĐHQGHN dated December 25, 2014 of Vietnam National University, Hanoi on promulgating the Regulations on undergraduate training at Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

*Pursuant to Decision No. 3626/QĐ-ĐHQGHN dated October 31, 2022 of Vietnam National University, Hanoi on promulgating the Regulations on undergraduate training at Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

*Pursuant to Decision No. 700/QĐ-ĐHVN dated September 30, 2021 of Vietnam-Japan University on promulgating the Regulations on plagiarism prevention in training and scientific research at Vietnam-Japan University, Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

Vietnam-Japan University, Vietnam National University, Hanoi (VJU) hereby provides the following guidance on the organization of undergraduate theses and graduation projects (hereinafter referred to as "thesis/project") for full-time undergraduate students at VJU:

## I. General information

### 1. Scope and applicability

- This Guidance governs the organization and implementation of theses/projects in undergraduate programs at VJU.
- It shall apply to relevant functional departments, Faculties, training programs (CTĐT), lecturers supervising theses/projects (hereinafter "supervisors") and full-time undergraduate students at VJU (hereinafter "students").

### 2. Objectives

- Define the timeline milestones for the thesis/project process.
- Specify the requirements throughout the thesis/project process in accordance with the VNU undergraduate training regulations applicable to each cohort and other regulations in force at VJU.
- Guide Faculties, training programs, supervisors and students in carrying out the related tasks according to the Thesis/Project Implementation Schedule (Appendix 1).

## II. Eligibility and student obligations

### 1. Eligibility to undertake a thesis/project

- Students shall not be under disciplinary suspension or criminal investigation, and shall be in compliance with the University's tuition payment regulations.
- At the time of registration: students shall have a cumulative GPA of at least 2.0 by the end of the third academic year and shall have completed the courses "Scientific Research Methodology" and "Professional Internship" in the program curriculum.
- At the time of defence: students shall have completed all major-specific courses in the curriculum (excluding the thesis/project course); shall obtain a plagiarism check result from the Department of Examination and Quality Assurance showing a duplication rate below 20%; and shall submit the defence application signed by the supervisor (Guidance Log, Appendix 3).
- Other cases shall be subject to approval by the Rectorate Board.

### 2. Student obligations

- Strictly comply with assignments from the University, maintain contact with the supervisor and carry out tasks as instructed.
- Complete and defend the thesis/project according to the Faculty's schedule.
- Students may only defend the thesis/project after VJU issues the decision authorizing the defence.

## III. Requirements and duties of supervisors

### 1. Professional requirements

- Supervisors shall be university lecturers or researchers holding at least a master's degree.
- Supervisors holding a master's degree shall have at least two years of university teaching experience and expertise/research relevant to the program's field.

### 2. Assignment of supervisors

- Each thesis/project may have one or two supervisors.
- Where two supervisors are assigned, their roles as principal supervisor and co-supervisor shall be clearly defined. At least one supervisor shall be affiliated with a unit within Vietnam National University, Hanoi.
- Supervisors holding only a master's degree shall not supervise independently.
- The maximum number of students a supervisor may advise per academic year depends on academic rank and qualifications, as follows:

| No. | Academic title/degree | Maximum number of students |
| :-- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Professor | 8 |
| 2 | Associate Professor or Doctor of Science | 7 |
| 3 | PhD holder or Senior Lecturer | 6 |
| 4 | Lecturer holding a master's degree with at least two years of relevant university teaching, professional and research experience | 2 |

- In special cases, the Rectorate Board shall decide the number of students a supervisor may advise in an academic year.

### 3. Supervisor duties

- Advise and provide feedback to students on thesis/project topics, outlines, research implementation and related matters.
- Monitor the entire thesis/project process for each student.
- Meet students at least eight times to provide guidance, answer questions and comment on issues related to the thesis/project. Discussions shall be recorded in the Guidance Log (Appendix 3).
- Provide written comments and assessments of the student's work (Guidance Log, Appendix 3).
- If a supervisor fails to fulfil their supervisory duties in accordance with regulations, VJU reserves the right to suspend their supervisory role and impose disciplinary measures.

## IV. Requirements for theses and graduation projects

### 1. Topics

- Students may select topics aligned with specialised directions in the program to address urgent and meaningful theoretical or practical issues related to the discipline.
- Theses/projects shall present original elements, employ clear and modern research methodologies to ensure accuracy and reliability, be concisely and coherently written, and produce results that could be published in academic journals or presented at peer-reviewed conferences.

### 2. Language

- Students may write and defend their thesis/project in Vietnamese, English or Japanese (unless specified otherwise in the curriculum).
- For high-quality programs, if the thesis/project is written in Vietnamese, a summary of at least two A4 pages (font size 14, Times New Roman) in English or Japanese shall be required.

### 3. Structure

- Cover page (see Appendix 2).
- Title page (see Appendix 2) signed by the supervisor.
- Declaration of originality (signed in blue ink with the student's full name).
- Acknowledgements (with the student's full name).
- Table of contents listing sections, chapters, headings and, if applicable, subheadings with page numbers aligned to the right margin.
- List of abbreviations (if any) arranged alphabetically; phrases of four or fewer characters or infrequently used phrases shall not be abbreviated.
- List of tables and figures.
- Summary of the thesis/project (details in Appendix 2).
- Introduction.
- Main content (chapters).
- Conclusions and recommendations.
- References (details in Appendix 2).
- Appendices.

### 4. Formatting

- The work shall be presented concisely and clearly, without grammatical or spelling errors and without erasures.
- Length: approximately 25–50 pages of main content for a thesis and 50–70 pages for a project (excluding references and appendices), formatted on A4 paper (210 × 297 mm).
- Font: size 13 or 14 without condensing or stretching letter spacing:
  + Vietnamese/English: Times New Roman
  + Japanese: MS Mincho or Yu Mincho
  + For documents containing many mathematical formulas, use typesetting software such as PCTeX or LaTeX.
- Line spacing: 1.5 lines.
- Margins: top 2.5 cm; bottom 3 cm; left 3.5 cm; right 2 cm.
- Paragraph spacing: 6 pt. The first line of each paragraph shall be indented by 1.27 cm.
- Page numbering: centred at the bottom of each page, numbered consecutively from the introduction through to the conclusion.
- Tables: numbered consecutively with captions placed above the table.
- Figures (including charts, diagrams and images): numbered consecutively throughout the thesis/project with captions placed below.
- References: detailed citation and formatting requirements are provided in Appendix 2.
- Chapter, section and subsection numbering:
  + Use Arabic numerals (do not use Roman numerals).
  + Sections and subsections shall follow the structure x.y.z, where x is the chapter number, y is the section number and z is the subsection number. For example, chapter 1 sections: 1.1, 1.2, 1.3; subsections: 1.1.1, 1.1.2, 1.2.1, etc.

## V. Thesis/project evaluation committee

### 1. Composition

- The Rector of VJU shall establish thesis/project evaluation committees based on recommendations from the Dean of the Faculty and the Head of the Department of Training and Student Affairs.
- Each committee shall comprise 5 to 7 members, including one chairperson, one secretary, two reviewers, a representative of the supervisor(s) and other members.

### 2. Member requirements

- The chairperson and reviewers shall hold a doctoral degree or higher and have at least two years of research and teaching experience.
- Other members shall hold at least a master's degree and have at least two years of research and teaching experience.

## VI. Organization of thesis/project assessment

### 1. Conditions for conducting the assessment

- The defence may proceed when at least two-thirds of committee members are physically present, including the chairperson, secretary and at least one reviewer.
- A representative of the Department of Training and Student Affairs shall attend the defence.
- Any absent member shall notify the chairperson; their score sheets and reviewer comments (if applicable) shall be sealed in an envelope, signed by the absent member and delivered to the chairperson.

### 2. Assessment criteria

- Based on the proposal from the training program, the Rector shall stipulate the assessment criteria for theses/projects.
- Criteria shall include content, presentation, defence performance and research achievements (if any). Faculties shall develop a marking scheme using a 10-point scale (rounded to one decimal place) and convert scores to letter grades in accordance with VNU's undergraduate training regulations.
- Scores shall be announced publicly at the defence session.
- Students receiving an F grade shall re-register for the thesis/project within two months of receiving the result. Only one re-registration shall be permitted. A second F grade shall result in ineligibility for graduation consideration.

### 3. Defence procedures

#### 3.1. In-person defence

- At least five working days before the defence: students shall submit two hard copies and a PDF of the thesis/project to the training program so committee members can review and comment.
- The Faculty shall send guidance documents and forms (score sheets and reviewer comment forms) together with the digital thesis/project to committee members.
- One day before the defence: the Faculty shall prepare facilities, equipment, documents and records for the session.
- The defence session shall proceed as follows:

Step 1: A Faculty representative states the purpose of the session, introduces guests (if any) and announces the list of presenting students.

Step 2: The committee secretary reads the decision establishing the committee, introduces the members and invites the chairperson to preside.

Step 3: The chairperson confirms attendance and, if quorum is met, directs the proceedings.

Step 4: The secretary invites the student to prepare for the presentation.

Step 5: The student presents a summary of the thesis/project within 10–15 minutes.

Step 6: The two reviewers deliver their assessments after the presentation. If a reviewer is absent, the secretary reads out their written review and score sheet as authorized by the chairperson.

Step 7: Committee members question the student, and the student responds.

Step 8: After all questions have been answered, the supervisor's representative provides comments on the student's work.

Step 9: The committee deliberates in private. Members record their scores on the score sheets and submit them to the secretary.

Step 10: The secretary consolidates the scores, completes the minutes and reads them aloud to the committee for confirmation before submitting them to the chairperson.

Step 11: The secretary publicly announces the committee's conclusions, including the defence score and required revisions, to all students in attendance.

#### 3.2. Online defence

- If an in-person defence is not possible, the defence may be held online provided that:
  + Approval is obtained from the VJU Rectorate Board.
  + The assessment is conducted by a specialised committee of at least five members.
  + All committee members and students consent to the online format.
  + The entire session is recorded (audio and video) and archived in accordance with regulations.
- The online defence shall follow the same steps as the in-person defence.

### 4. Submission of theses/projects

#### 4.1. Student revisions and submission

- Students shall revise the thesis/project based on the committee's conclusions and submit the digital file to the Faculty for forwarding to the Department of Examination and Quality Assurance for a final plagiarism check. If the similarity index is 20% or higher, the student shall continue revising as instructed. If it is below 20%, the student shall complete the Revision Confirmation Form (Appendix 3) signed by the student, supervisor and committee chairperson.
- Within three working days from the official announcement of the score, students shall submit the revised thesis/project in person: two hard copies, one complete PDF and one PDF summary. The Revision Confirmation Form shall be bound into the final page of the hard copy.

#### 4.2. Faculty compilation

- Faculties shall compile students' files (full thesis/project PDFs and summary PDFs) into folders named "Program_Student Name_Student ID" (see the summary compilation template for Faculties in Appendix 1.5).
- Faculties shall send all files to the Department of Training and Student Affairs at graduation_ug@st.vju.ac.vn.
- Faculties shall submit hard-copy score sheets signed by the committee chairperson and secretary to the Department of Training and Student Affairs.
