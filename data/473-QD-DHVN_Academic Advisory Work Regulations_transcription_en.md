# REGULATIONS

<p align="center"><strong>ON ACADEMIC ADVISORY WORK AT VIETNAM-JAPAN UNIVERSITY, VIETNAM NATIONAL UNIVERSITY, HANOI</strong></p>

*(Issued with Decision No. 473/QĐ-ĐHVN dated May 20, 2025 of the Rector of Vietnam-Japan University, VNU)*

# Chapter I. General Provisions

## Article 1. Scope of regulation and applicable subjects

This document stipulates a number of contents on academic advisory work for full-time undergraduate students at Vietnam-Japan University (VJU), Vietnam National University, Hanoi (VNU).
This document applies to lecturers assigned as academic advisors and related units, organizations and individuals in VJU.

## Article 2. Purpose

1. Support and assist students in performing well in their studies and training tasks; advise on study orientation, scientific research and career for students throughout their time at the University.
2. Help VJU, Faculty/Academic Program grasp information on students' learning situation to have appropriate support measures.
3. Serve as one of the criteria for evaluating and classifying lecturers annually according to current regulations.
4. Strengthen the connection between the University, lecturers, students and students' families in student management and support.

## Article 3. Requirements for academic advisors

1. An academic advisor shall be a lecturer with at least 2 years of working experience in the assigned related field. Special cases shall be decided by the Board of Directors of VJU.
2. Academic advisors are in charge of each cohort class (each advisor is in charge of no more than 2 cohort classes) and are assigned at the beginning of the cohort. The total number of students under an advisor's charge shall not exceed 100 students.
3. The term of office of the academic advisor is determined by cohort, unless otherwise adjusted.

# Chapter II. Responsibilities, Powers, and Benefits of Academic Advisors

## Article 4. Responsibilities of academic advisors

Academic advisors are responsible for performing tasks according to the following four main functional groups:
    a) Support for study and research
- Guide students to understand the regulations and guidelines on university training of VNU and VJU, especially the contents related to study plans, course registration, academic conditions and graduation.
- Guide students to build and update personal study plans; advise on choosing courses suitable to their abilities, aspirations and requirements of the training program.
- Guide students on effective study methods, scientific research skills and critical thinking; encourage students to participate in academic activities and seminars.
- Monitor students' learning outcomes, early detect cases showing signs of learning decline to coordinate with functional units to handle and support promptly.
    b) Personal and career development
- Advise and introduce students to scholarship opportunities, academic exchange programs, internships, research participation, advanced courses or career-oriented elective courses.
- Accompany students in developing soft skills (study skills, communication, teamwork, time management, etc.), career orientation and choosing a development path after graduation.
- Coordinate with units in the university to develop or propose skills topics, extracurricular activities, experiential learning, to comprehensively develop students.
    c) Connecting and coordinating organization
- Support the Director/Program Coordinator in organizing learning activities, seminars, professional activities, and periodic class activities for each semester/academic year.
- Contact students' families when necessary to coordinate support for students in terms of learning, psychology, learning conditions or other arising situations.
- Coordinate with the Department of Training and Student Affairs, Faculty/Academic Program and related units in evaluating training scores, considering scholarships, handling academic affairs, managing classes, urging students to conduct surveys of the University and related work.
    d) Technology application and reporting
- Effectively use support tools such as learning management systems (LMS), survey forms, learning progress tracking tables, etc. to enhance interaction and manage student information.
- Fully implement the periodic reporting regime (by semester/academic year) and ad hoc reports on class situations, academic advisory results as required by the University or functional units.

## Article 5. Powers of academic advisors

1. Request the Faculty/Academic Program and leaders of functional departments to provide necessary documents and information related to academic advisory work.
2. Require the Class Executive Committee to periodically or promptly report on all aspects of the class situation to have timely guidance and advice on student activities; propose recognition/removal of the Class Executive Committee.
3. Propose rewards and discipline for students; participate in discussions and propose opinions at Faculty/University meetings related to students and classes in charge.
4. Sign the confirmation on the course registration/withdrawal forms.
5. Participate in training and share advisory experiences at internal training sessions to encourage the academic advisory community to develop capacity.

## Article 6. Time and content of working with cohort classes, students/student groups

1. Working time
- For cohort classes: Each academic year, the academic advisor organizes at least two official working sessions with the class, each lasting at least 60 minutes. (Sample class meeting minutes in Appendix 1).
- For students/student groups: Working time with students is confirmed according to the Table of Determining Completed Workload (Form in Appendix 2).
- The total working time of the academic advisor is determined according to the Internal Expenditure Regulations.

2. Work content
- The content of work with classes, students/student groups is reported to the Faculty according to the form in Appendices 1, 2.
- The academic advisor is responsible for publicly announcing to the Faculty, University and students the form of contact (in person, phone, email, LMS, etc.), location and time of receiving students so that the academic advisory work can take place smoothly and effectively.

## Article 7. Benefits of academic advisors

1. Enjoy benefits according to current regulations on working regime and internal spending regulations of the University.
2. Time is arranged to attend professional training courses.
3. Priority is given to attend training courses and seminars to develop advisory skills.

## Article 8. Rewards and discipline

1. Completion of academic advisory tasks is one of the annual emulation and reward criteria.
2. Academic advisors who complete their tasks well and excellently shall be considered for commendation according to the University's regulations.
3. Academic advisors who do not complete their tasks shall be subject to disciplinary action according to the University's regulations.

## Article 9. Students' responsibilities for academic advising

1. Proactively contact the academic advisor when advice is needed on studying, registering for courses, career orientation or any difficulties that arise.
2. Fully and seriously participate in class meetings and work with the academic advisor as planned.
3. Provide honest information and cooperate during communication with the academic advisor to ensure effective and timely support.
4. Provide feedback on academic advisory activities upon request from the Faculty/Academic Program or the University.

# Chapter III. Implementation Organization

## Article 10. Implementation

1. The Faculty Board organizes the selection and proposes a list of academic advisors by cohort class to the Board of Directors (through the Department of Training and Student Affairs). In case the academic advisor cannot take over, the Faculty Board shall select a qualified lecturer to replace them.
2. The Faculty Board organizes regular meetings (monthly, quarterly or semesterly) with the academic advisors to promptly grasp the learning situation and policies of students and propose opinions to the University for decision.
3. The academic advisor submits the Workload Determination Table to the Faculty Board within 02 weeks from the end of the academic year.
4. The University periodically organizes training, exchanges experiences and evaluates the results of academic advisory activities; commends academic advisors with positive contributions.
5. Heads of units and relevant organizations and individuals are responsible for organizing the implementation of this regulation.
6. During the implementation process, if there are any problems or adjustments needed, units and individuals shall report to the Rector (through the Department of Training and Student Affairs) for consideration, revision and supplementation.
