# REGULATIONS

**On the Prevention of Plagiarism in Education and Scientific Research at VNU Vietnam Japan University**
*(Promulgated in conjunction with Decision No. 700 dated September 30, 2021, of the Rector of VNU Vietnam Japan University)*

# Chapter I. GENERAL PROVISIONS

## Article 1. Scope and Subjects of Application

1. This document provides for the prevention of plagiarism in education and scientific research at Vietnam Japan University (hereinafter referred to as "the University").

2. This document applies to undergraduate students, graduate students, and doctoral candidates (hereinafter collectively referred to as "students") in all levels and programs of study at the University.

## Article 2. Definition of Terms

In this document, the following terms shall be construed as follows:

1. **Plagiarism** means the use of another person's words, ideas, or research results in academic activities as if they were one's own. Plagiarism includes: paraphrasing, presenting, reproducing, or translating another person's text or ideas without proper citation; using all or part of another person's paper (including commercial ones sold on the market); using another person's artistic works, designs, charts, or data without attribution; and self-plagiarism (using more than 30% of the content of one's own previously published papers or scientific research results for publication at another institution).

2. **Cheating** means the act of using or preparing to use false, fraudulent, or deceptive means, or making false representations, presentations, or declarations regarding one's own academic activities. Cheating includes: using books, materials, notes, electronic devices, or other aids without permission; conversing or exchanging information during examinations, tests, or other academic activities; collaborating with others on homework, reports, programming, or other assignments without the instructor's permission; the act of stealing examination questions or answers before an exam; and the act of creating or using fraudulent documents.

3. **Facilitating dishonesty** means the act of helping or preparing to help another person commit an act of plagiarism. Facilitating dishonesty includes: taking an exam for another person, or requesting/hiring another person to take an exam on one's behalf; preparing reports or research for another person; and allowing another person to copy one's answers during an examination, test, or other academic activity.

4. **Fabrication** means the act of intentionally misrepresenting or inventing information in any academic activity. Fabrication includes the use of fabricated information in laboratory, research, or practical training reports, or other academic activities.

## Article 3. Purpose of Promulgating the Regulations on the Prevention of Plagiarism in Education and Scientific Research

Relevant departments, organizations, and individuals within the University shall use these Regulations to understand the guidelines for preventing and combating plagiarism and related violations, as well as the procedures and forms of handling violations when they occur.

# Chapter II. PREVENTION OF PLAGIARISM IN EDUCATION AND SCIENTIFIC RESEARCH

## Article 4. Prevention of Plagiarism

1. Relevant departments, organizations, and individuals within the University must understand citation methods, comply with regulations on scientific citation, and avoid plagiarism.

2. All opinions, insightful concepts, and all other references that are not the author's own must be cited (with the source indicated) to ensure transparency. The main purposes of citing references are to identify the sources of valuable ideas and to demonstrate the author's integrity, knowledge, and ability to process information.

3. All graduation theses, master's theses, doctoral dissertations, and scientific research results must undergo a plagiarism check before being graded or before the results are announced.

4. A pledge must be written before publishing the results of a graduation thesis, master's thesis, doctoral dissertation, or scientific research.

To prevent and deter acts of plagiarism, the author of a graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation or scientific research outcome must include the following pledge at the beginning of the assignment, report, graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation, or scientific research outcome:

> I have read and understood plagiarism violations. I pledge on my personal honor that this research is my own work and does not violate the Regulations on the Prevention of Plagiarism in Education and Scientific Research at VNU Vietnam Japan University (promulgated in conjunction with Decision No. .../QĐ-ĐHVN of .../.../.... by the Rector of Vietnam Japan University).

## Article 5. Storage and Confidentiality of Plagiarism Check Results

The results of the duplication check and the original database are the responsibility of the University's Office of Examination and Quality Assurance to store in accordance with national and University regulations.

## Article 6. Plagiarism Check Tools, Subjects, and Fees

1. The University provides the Turnitin system or software with equivalent functionality approved by the Rector for checking spelling and detecting duplication.

2. The University's Office of Examination and Quality Assurance shall receive the technology, and fully update and provide guidance to staff and students of the University participating in scientific research and educational activities both inside and outside the University.

3. All graduation theses, master's theses, doctoral dissertations, and scientific research results must undergo a plagiarism check using the Turnitin software or equivalent software for checking spelling and detecting duplication, in accordance with the procedure described in Article 7 of these Regulations, before being graded or before the results are announced. For graduation theses/master's theses/doctoral dissertations: an anti-plagiarism check must be conducted before the defense. For doctoral dissertations: an anti-plagiarism check must be conducted twice, once before the defense at the base level and once before the defense at the VNU level.

4. Based on the plan, financial situation, and the internal spending regulations as amended annually, Vietnam Japan University may regulate the fees payable by students when using an account for the Turnitin spelling and duplication detection software or equivalent software.

## Article 7. Plagiarism Check Procedure

1. **Procedure for plagiarism check of graduation theses/master's theses/doctoral dissertations**
    a. **Step 1:** Each student and instructor is granted one account to access the Turnitin spelling and duplication detection system or equivalent software. Students may use this account throughout their period of study and research at the University.
    b. **Step 2:** Students access the system to self-check the similarity level of their graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation, either independently or at the request of their supervisor.
    c. **Step 3:** Students report the similarity check results to their supervisor and revise the thesis to ensure that the similarity in words and sentences does not exceed 20% before proceeding with the application for the defense of the graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation. Students are solely responsible for their own check results.
    d. **Step 4:** Before the official defense of a graduation thesis or master's thesis by a student or graduate student, and before a doctoral candidate's defense at the base level, the student must submit their graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation to the University's Office of Examination and Quality Assurance for a plagiarism check.
    e. **Step 5:** The University's Office of Examination and Quality Assurance conducts a plagiarism check using Turnitin software or equivalent software and issues a plagiarism check result certificate to the student (if the similarity in words and sentences does not exceed 20%).
    f. **Step 6:** The student submits the plagiarism check result certificate along with the complete application documents for the defense to the document reception desk of the responsible department to proceed with the defense procedures.
    g. **Step 7:** Based on the submitted documents and the plagiarism check result certificate, the Rector decides on the implementation of the student's graduation defense.
    h. **Step 8:** In the case of doctoral candidates: after revising the dissertation based on the opinions of the base-level dissertation review committee and before defending it before the VNU-level committee, Steps 4, 5, and 6 must be repeated.
    i. **Step 9:** If, after a student has completed their graduation defense, there is a complaint or accusation of plagiarism, or if plagiarism is discovered through an inspection by VNU Hanoi, the student shall bear full responsibility and be disciplined in accordance with the University's Regulations on the Prevention of Plagiarism in Education and Scientific Research.
    j. **Step 10:** Storage and Confidentiality: The Office of Examination and Quality Assurance is responsible for storing the plagiarism check results in accordance with national and University regulations.

2. **Procedure for plagiarism check of final exams, essays, assignment reports, and practical training reports**
    a. **For administrative offices:** Annually, based on the end-of-semester examination plan, the Office of Examination and Quality Assurance, in coordination with the Department of Inspection and Legal Affairs, shall develop an inspection plan for the University's anti-plagiarism activities by checking the plagiarism level of students' final exams, essays, assignment reports, and practical training reports. If any plagiarism violations are discovered, they shall be reported to the Rector's Office for handling at a level similar to that specified in Article 9 of these Regulations.
    b. **For instructors:** If an instructor suspects plagiarism by a student during the grading process of final exams, essays, assignment reports, practical training reports, etc., they may self-check the student's plagiarism through Turnitin software or equivalent software, or request the Office of Examination and Quality Assurance to check the plagiarism level, and handle the violation as specified in Article 9 of these Regulations.
    c. **For students:** Students are provided with an account for Turnitin software or equivalent software to self-check their own plagiarism as needed. Self-checking of plagiarism by students for final exams, essays, assignment reports, and practical training reports is not mandatory. However, if plagiarism is discovered, it will be considered for disciplinary action in the same manner as in Article 9 of these Regulations.

# Chapter III. HANDLING OF PLAGIARISM VIOLATIONS IN EDUCATION AND SCIENTIFIC RESEARCH

## Article 8. Principles of Disciplinary Action

1. Relevant departments, organizations, and individuals within the University shall strictly implement these Regulations in a spirit of openness and transparency.

2. All cases shall be handled without bias.

3. All cases of suspected or accused plagiarism violations must be processed immediately upon discovery and when there is sufficient evidence.

## Article 9. Disciplinary Actions for Violations

1. **Violations in the examination room**
    The proctor shall create a record of the violation in the examination room and take disciplinary action. For undergraduate students: disciplinary action shall be taken in accordance with Article 32 of the Regulations on Bachelor's Level Education at VNU Hanoi (promulgated in conjunction with Decision No. 5115/QĐ-ĐHQGHN dated December 25, 2014, of the President of VNU Hanoi). For graduate students: disciplinary action shall be taken in accordance with Article 52 of the Regulations on Master's Level Education at VNU Hanoi (promulgated in conjunction with Decision No. 4668/QĐ-ĐHQGHN dated December 10, 2014, of the President of VNU Hanoi), and Article 68 of the Regulations on Graduate Education at VNU Hanoi (as amended and supplemented by Decision No. 1555/QĐ-ĐHQGHN dated May 25, 2011, and Decision No. 3050/QĐ-ĐHQGHN dated September 17, 2012, of the President of VNU Hanoi).

2. **Plagiarism**
    a. In cases where there is an accusation of plagiarism in a graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation or other scientific research, or if a supervisor or reviewer suspects/discovers plagiarism, the receiving officer/department shall send the documents to the University's Office of Examination and Quality Assurance for a plagiarism check using Turnitin software or equivalent software. If the check confirms plagiarism, disciplinary action shall be taken according to the regulations, based on the extent and severity of the violation.
    b. If a check using spelling and duplication detection software shows a plagiarism error of not more than 20% and the citation of references is not in accordance with the regulations, the author is required to revise the cited parts in accordance with the requirements of Section 5, item 5.5 and Appendix 14 of the Guidance No. 45/ĐT on the Implementation of Doctoral Dissertation Evaluation at VNU Hanoi (dated February 27, 2003).
    c. If a check using spelling and duplication detection software shows a plagiarism error of more than 20% but less than 30%, the author is required to rewrite the graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation and provide citations in accordance with the requirements of Section 5, item 5.5 and Appendix 14 of the Guidance No. 45/ĐT on the Implementation of Doctoral Dissertation Evaluation at VNU Hanoi (dated February 27, 2003). At the same time, the instructor will decide whether to deduct points from the scientific research result (deducted according to the severity of the violation, but not exceeding 25% of the score for that research result), and a record of reprimand within the University will be created.
    d. If a check using spelling and duplication detection software shows a plagiarism error of 30% or more but less than 50%, the author is required to rewrite the graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation and provide citations in accordance with the requirements of Section 5, item 5.5 and Appendix 14 of the Guidance No. 45/ĐT on the Implementation of Doctoral Dissertation Evaluation at VNU Hanoi (dated February 27, 2003). At the same time, the instructor will deduct 50% of the points from the scientific research result, and the student will be subject to a warning or a one-year suspension from the University.
    e. If a check using spelling and duplication detection software shows a plagiarism error of 50% or more, the instructor will assign a score of 0. At the same time, the student will be subject to disciplinary action ranging from a one-year suspension to expulsion.
    f. For facilitating another person's plagiarism violation: If the person is a current student of the University, they will be suspended for one year or expelled. If the person is not a current student of the University, the University's Department of Academic and Student Affairs will notify the person's place of residence/study of the violation and request disciplinary action.
    g. For final exams, essays, assignment reports, and practical training reports, if there is an accusation of plagiarism or if an instructor suspects plagiarism, the department receiving the accusation documents shall send them to the University's Office of Examination and Quality Assurance for a plagiarism check using Turnitin software or equivalent software. If the check confirms plagiarism, disciplinary action shall be taken according to the regulations, based on the extent and severity of the violation. Specifically:
  - If the plagiarism error is more than 20% but less than 30%, the student must retake the final exam, and 10% will be deducted from the exam score.
  - If the plagiarism is 30% or more but less than 50%, the student must retake the final exam, and 25% will be deducted from the exam score.
  - If the plagiarism is 50% or more, the instructor will assign a score of 0, and the student must retake the course.

3. **Cheating and Fabrication**
    a. If a student commits cheating by using a false certificate or transcript to apply for a course/exam exemption, the Rector will decide on disciplinary action ranging from a one-year suspension to expulsion, depending on the documents and the severity of the violation.
    b. If a student commits cheating by using a false graduation certificate (high school/vocational school/college/university/graduate school) to apply for admission to an academic program, the Rector will decide on the removal of the student's name from the admission decision, or the removal of the name from the graduation decision and the revocation of the degree and academic transcript (if applicable), and if necessary, send the documents to the police authorities for consideration and handling.
    c. If a student commits cheating by forging a transcript/certificate/diploma issued by the University: If the student is a current student of the University, they will be expelled, the forged certificate will be confiscated, and the documents will be sent to the competent police authority for resolution. If the person is not a current student of the University, the University's Department of Academic and Student Affairs will send the documents to the competent police authority for resolution or notify the person's place of residence/study of the violation and request disciplinary action.
    d. If a student commits cheating, attends classes on behalf of another or has another person attend on their behalf, obtains exam questions or answers before an exam, organizes surrogate learning, examination, or testing, organizes surrogate writing of a graduation thesis/master's thesis, or other scientific research, or commits other acts of cheating, they will be disciplined in accordance with Article 12, Clause 1 of these Regulations, or the documents will be sent to the police authorities for handling if necessary.
    e. If a student commits fabrication: If an instructor confirms a student's fabrication, the instructor will assign a score of 0 for that subject and create a record of warning within the University.

## Article 10. Procedure for Handling Plagiarism Violations

1. **When a graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation is confirmed to have a plagiarism violation**
    a. If the author of the graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation admits to the act of plagiarism, the person who discovered the plagiarism/the person responsible for handling the act of plagiarism is responsible for creating a record signed by the violator, the supervisor, and the Head of the University's Office of Examination and Quality Assurance, and handling the case in accordance with Article 9 of these Regulations.
    b. If the author of the graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation does not admit to their act of plagiarism, the Head of the University's Office of Examination and Quality Assurance will convene a meeting with the Quality Assurance Institute of VNU Hanoi, the Information and Library Center, and the author of the said thesis to determine whether it is plagiarism. If it is determined to be plagiarism, it will be handled in accordance with Article 9 of these Regulations. If it cannot be determined to be plagiarism, it will be handled in accordance with Article 10, Clause 2 of these Regulations.

2. **When it cannot be determined whether a graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation is a plagiarism violation**
    a. The relevant department will send the documents to the Scientific and Academic Council of the home unit for consideration and resolution.
    b. If the Scientific and Academic Council concludes that the author of the graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation has not committed a plagiarism violation, it will notify the discoverer within 7 days from the date the Scientific and Academic Council of the home unit issues its official conclusion.
    c. If the Scientific and Academic Council concludes that the author of the graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation has committed a plagiarism violation, it will be handled in accordance with Article 9 of these Regulations.
    d. If, after the Scientific and Academic Council of the home unit has issued its final conclusion, a complaint or accusation arises, or if the author of the graduation thesis/master's thesis/doctoral dissertation does not agree with the conclusion of the Scientific and Academic Council of the home unit, they may file a complaint with the Scientific and Academic Council of VNU Hanoi for resolution. The decision of the Scientific and Academic Council of VNU Hanoi shall be the final decision.

3. **The procedure for handling plagiarism is processed immediately upon discovery and when there is sufficient evidence.** Violators of plagiarism will be disciplined even if their course grades have been previously recognized or if they have already graduated.

# Chapter IV. IMPLEMENTATION ORGANIZATION

## Article 11. Office of Examination and Quality Assurance

The Office of Examination and Quality Assurance is the central unit responsible for guiding, inspecting, supervising, and annually reporting to the Rector on the implementation status and results of these Regulations, in coordination with the various offices.

## Article 12. Offices, Institutes, Faculties/Academic Programs, and Other Relevant Parties

1. Strictly implement the Regulations on the Prevention of Plagiarism in Education and Scientific Research at the University.

2. Based on these Regulations, the offices, institutes/faculties/academic programs shall regularly and continuously conduct training and provide specific guidance on plagiarism prevention to relevant organizations and individuals, maintaining a healthy, rigorous, and transparent academic environment at the University.

# Chapter V. IMPLEMENTATION CLAUSE

## Article 13. Effective Date

1. These Regulations shall take effect from the date of signing.

2. During the implementation process, these Regulations may be adjusted and amended to suit the situation. Amendments and supplements will be considered and decided by the Rector based on the proposal of the Head of the Office of Examination and Quality Assurance.
