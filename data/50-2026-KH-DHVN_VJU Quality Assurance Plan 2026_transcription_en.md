<p align="center"><strong>FOR IMPLEMENTING QUALITY ASSURANCE ACTIVITIES AT VIETNAM JAPAN UNIVERSITY IN 2026</strong></p>

<p align="center"><strong>1. Objectives</strong></p>
<p align="center"><strong>1.1. General objective</strong></p>
To build and operate a modern, effective, and sustainable internal quality assurance (IQA) system that meets the post-accreditation requirements for institutional accreditation under JUAA international standards.

<p align="center"><strong>1.2. Specific objectives</strong></p>
<p align="center"><strong>1.2.1. Quality accreditation and international standards</strong></p>
  - Maintain accreditation validity and fully implement all post-accreditation quality improvement requirements for institutional accreditation under the JUAA common international standards throughout the 7-year cycle (2025–2032).

<p align="center"><strong>1.2.2. Digital transformation in quality assurance</strong></p>
  - Build and operate an integrated faculty information system to support the collection, management, and analysis of data on staff quality assurance conditions.
  - Standardize the facilities data system to support the development and operation of an integrated facilities information system.
  - Apply data analytics and digital tools to monitor training quality, learning outcomes, and stakeholder feedback.

<p align="center"><strong>1.2.3. Development of the internal quality assurance (IQA) system</strong></p>
To improve the University's IQA model in the following direction:
  - Align it with AUN-QA standards, JUAA requirements, and other international standards;
  - Strengthen monitoring, evaluation, and continuous improvement (PDCA) mechanisms at all management and training levels.

<p align="center"><strong>1.2.4. Development of quality assurance information and evidence systems</strong></p>
  - Improve templates, focal points, and evidence collection procedures for self-assessment, external assessment, and quality improvement activities.
  - Enhance data/evidence readiness, traceability, and sharing capacity among units across the University for quality governance.

<p align="center"><strong>1.2.5. Development of quality culture and human resources</strong></p>
  - Build a quality culture across the entire University, in which every staff member, lecturer, and learner participates in and takes responsibility for quality.
  - Provide quality assurance, accreditation, and quality improvement training to 10% of staff, lecturers, and employees.

<p align="center"><strong>2. Quality assurance indicators to be achieved</strong></p>
  - **Improvement and development of the internal quality assurance system**: Review, update, and improve documents, policies, and regulations related to quality assurance in line with the new Strategy; strengthen and consolidate the organizational structure and assignment of responsibilities for quality assurance implementation at all levels.
  - **Quality accreditation**: Conduct self-assessment and external assessment for 01 undergraduate program and 01 master's program according to the AUN-QA 4.0 standards; complete the self-assessment of 01 undergraduate program according to the AUN-QA 4.0 standards.
  - **Quality improvement**: Implement the institutional quality improvement plan and carry out post-accreditation recommendations; develop 03 quality improvement reports for 3 accredited training programs; and 01 quality improvement report for the University.
  - **University rankings**: Provide full ranking data as required by VNU.
  - **Quality evaluation**:
    - 100% of courses are surveyed and collect learner feedback.
    - 100% of learners are surveyed regarding training support activities, with at least 30% of learners responding.
    - 100% of lecturers are surveyed regarding teaching activities and administrative staff, with 70% of lecturers completing self-evaluation of teaching activities and 50% of lecturers providing feedback on administrative staff.
    - 100% of staff are surveyed regarding leadership and management, with 50% of staff responding.
    - 100% of graduates one year after graduation are surveyed regarding employment status and achievement of program learning outcomes, with 50% of graduates responding.
    - Each training program collects at least 5 employer feedback responses regarding the achievement of graduate learning outcomes.
  - **Staff development**: Develop a detailed personnel recruitment plan for each training program in order to recruit 50% of the required staffing needs of the training programs.
