# GUIDANCE

**On practical internship and internship topics for full-time undergraduate students at Vietnam-Japan University**

*Pursuant to Decision No. 1186/QĐ-TTg dated July 21, 2014 of the Prime Minister on the establishment of Vietnam-Japan University under Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

*Pursuant to Decision No. 391/QĐ-TTg dated March 31, 2017 of the Prime Minister on the Regulations on organization and operation of Vietnam-Japan University, Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

*Pursuant to Decision No. 5115/QĐ-ĐHQGHN dated December 25, 2014 of the President of VNU on promulgating the Regulations on undergraduate training at Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

*Pursuant to Decision No. 3626/QĐ-ĐHQGHN dated November 24, 2022 of the President of VNU on promulgating the Regulations on undergraduate training at Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

*Pursuant to Guidance No. 1179/HD-ĐHQGHN dated April 20, 2020 of the President of VNU on practical internship and internship topics for full-time undergraduate students at Vietnam National University, Hanoi;*

Vietnam-Japan University hereby provides guidance to the Faculties on the implementation of practical internship and internship topics for full-time students as follows:

## 1. Scope of regulation and applicable subjects

- This Guidance provides for the implementation of practical internship activities, reporting and evaluation of practical internship activities.
- This Guidance shall apply to students and lecturers of training programs (CTĐT) at Vietnam-Japan University.

## 2. Principles for ensuring the quality and effectiveness of internship activities

- Meet the learning outcomes of the training program through practical experience activities.
- Encourage students' autonomy and creativity, increasing employment opportunities during study and after graduation.
- Provide opportunities for students to demonstrate their competence, analyze and evaluate work, enhance self-confidence, personal orientation and establish social skills.
- Help students become familiar with organizations, develop awareness of social relationships and intercultural communication skills in a multicultural environment.
- Innovate teaching methods, combine training between the University and internship facilities to improve adaptability and employability.

## 3. Practical internship training program

- The practical internship component is mandatory within the framework structure of the training program. Students shall undergo at least one semester combining classroom learning with practical activities and internships at the internship facility.
- Practical and internship activities shall be carried out continuously and flexibly throughout the students' training process at the University.
- Internship activities shall be carried out flexibly in terms of time, provided that the total internship time and study time do not exceed the maximum total duration of the course as prescribed by current training regulations.
- The organization of practical internships for students shall be conducted systematically, with close coordination and connection between the training unit and the practice and internship facilities.
- Internship and practical activities may be deployed on the basis of applying advanced technology, laboratory simulation, and field observation visits.
- Training programs in the fields of economics, law, education, foreign languages, social sciences and humanities shall include a practical internship component at internship facilities.
- Training programs in natural sciences, technology and engineering, health and life sciences shall include practical internship components at laboratories, workshops, factories, enterprises, hospitals and centers.
- The duration of practical internship shall be converted in the same manner as laboratory practice time. One credit hour of practical training is equivalent to 3–6 internship sessions at the facility. To complete one credit hour of practical training, students shall devote at least 2 standard sessions of personal preparation.
- Students may undertake internships in multiple periods throughout the entire course, provided that the total assessed duration is at least 30 working days throughout the entire course, at times suitable to the student's personal schedule and the needs of the receiving unit (or individual).

## 4. Requirements for internship facilities

- Internship facilities are enterprises, factories, workshops, corporations, socio-political organizations, schools, research institutes, and other internship facilities related to the student's field of study.
- Internship facilities shall be legally compliant (possessing business registration, registered address for entities that are enterprises or organizations, or having educational and training functions) to ensure the physical, personal and mental safety of students.
- Internship facilities shall meet the requirements of professional training content, knowledge and professional skill development, and necessary supplementary skills for students to achieve the learning outcomes of the training program.
- Internship facilities shall assign staff with appropriate professional qualifications and provide suitable physical facilities to support students' internship activities; and shall support and create conditions for staff and students to participate in study and practical internship.
- Internship facilities shall share scientific and technological databases, library information, teaching materials, and data related to students' practical internship activities.
- Students who complete practical and internship activities at internship facilities shall obtain confirmation of internship completion from the internship facility.

## 5. Student internship data and results

- Information on students' practical internship results and data of the internship facility used during the internship period shall be treated as confidential information; relevant parties shall not disclose such information to other entities without the written consent of the relevant parties.
- Students' practical internship results shall be used to evaluate the achievement of course learning outcomes in the training program.
- Internship data, research, inventions and patents of students during the practical internship shall be the intellectual property of Vietnam-Japan University unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## 6. Internship registration

- Conditions for students to register for internship:
  + Having completed at least 14 credits of the disciplinary and field-specific knowledge block of the training program.
  + Satisfying the prerequisites of the internship course.
  + Not being subject to criminal prosecution or not being under disciplinary suspension from study.
  + Having fully fulfilled tuition obligations as prescribed by Vietnam-Japan University.
- Internship registration procedures:
  + The University shall organize for students to register for internship courses by semester on the course registration system.
  + The Faculty shall submit the list of students eligible for internship, internship facilities and (prospective) supervisors to the Department of Training and Student Affairs.

## 7. Requirements for students

- Clearly understand the purpose and content of the internship.
- Comply with the assignments of the Faculty and supervisors, and perform assigned tasks.
- Comply with the regulations of the internship facility.
- Fully implement the internship plan and diary, meeting the progress requirements and regulations at the internship facility.
- Report any issues arising during the internship to the supervisor.
- Students may undertake internships at facilities recommended by their supervisors or may arrange their own internship placement. Students who arrange their own internship shall ensure the safety and suitability of the facility, or transfer to another location if requirements are not met.
- Students may accumulate internship activities from educational exchange programs at overseas training institutions.
- Submit the internship completion confirmation form with confirmation from the internship facility (Appendix 4).

## 8. Requirements for supervisors

- Supervisor qualifications: Shall meet the requirements regarding teaching experience or professional qualifications and shall cooperate with the internship facility in evaluation.
- Number of students supervised: A maximum of 15 students per internship period and no more than 8 students for thematic internship/graduation course per semester.
- Prepare the list of interns, internship facilities and supervisors (Appendix 2).
- Assist students in contacting internship facilities.
- Sign the internship diary confirmation (Appendix 3).
- Supervision content:
  + Review the plan and evaluate according to the Internship Diary.
  + Assist students in data collection, report writing, and research topic registration.
  + Review topics, preliminary and detailed outlines.
  + Edit the internship thesis manuscript.
  + Verify and approve academic integrity and objectivity.
  + Coordinate with internship facilities to evaluate students and grade internship reports.
- Coordinate with internship facilities to inspect and monitor the internship situation and receive payment in accordance with the internal spending regulations of Vietnam-Japan University.

## 9. Requirements for the comprehensive internship report

- Content: The comprehensive internship report is the outcome of the internship process, comprising information, documents, and practical activities during the internship. The main content of the report shall include:
  + General knowledge of the organization and operations of the enterprise where the internship was conducted.
  + Basic functional activities of the enterprise such as: production, business, finance, organization and human resources, etc.
  + Specialized knowledge directly related to the student's field of training.
- Format and presentation: Depending on the internship content of the training program, the Faculties shall specify the format and presentation of the internship report, which shall be at least 20 A4 pages (excluding appendices).

## 10. Evaluation of the comprehensive internship report results

- Internship reports shall be graded on a 10-point scale, rounded to one decimal place.
- The internship course may replace certain other courses in the training program if approved by the Rectorate Board.
- Students in the following cases shall receive a score of 0, shall retake the internship course and pay tuition:
  + Not attending the internship facility, not meeting with the supervisor, or not completing the Internship Diary.
  + Unexcused absence exceeding 20% of the time at the internship facility.
  + Copying or allowing others to copy the internship report.
- Cases of point deduction:
  + Deduction of 1 point for submission 3 days late, and 2 points for submission 4 or more days late.
  + Deduction of 2 points for violating regulations at the internship unit introduced by Vietnam-Japan University or for leaving the internship unit without authorization.

## 11. Responsibilities of relevant parties

### 11.1. Department of Training and Student Affairs

- Organize for students to register for internship courses on the course registration system according to the Faculty's plan.
- Issue letters of introduction for students to undertake internships if the internship facility so requires.
- Resolve issues arising in relation to students' internships.
- Publish internship course grades on the Undergraduate Training Information Portal.
- Support and coordinate in confirming teaching hours for lecturers participating in practical internship activities.

### 11.2. Department of Research Promotion, Cooperation and Development

- Establish relationships and organize cooperation agreements with enterprises and organizations to create internship opportunities for students.
- Provide a list of partners available for student internship placements to supervisors.

### 11.3. Department of Examination and Quality Assurance

- Provide guidance on and develop procedures for evaluating students' internship and practical results in accordance with the examination regulations of Vietnam-Japan University and VNU.
- Ensure that the quality of internships is consistent with the criteria of the training program.
- Collect feedback from students and enterprises to improve the internship program.

### 11.4. Department of Planning and Finance

- Support the payment of expenses related to the organization of internship activities in accordance with the internal spending regulations, including: remuneration for supervisors, student support funds, and organization of practical internships (if any).

### 11.5. Faculties

- Develop specialized internship content aligned with the learning outcomes of the course, and assign supervisors to guide students during the internship.
- Coordinate with enterprises and organizations to implement internship plans for students.
- Monitor and evaluate students' internship results, and propose improvements if necessary.
- Coordinate with relevant functional departments in organizing internship and practical activities.
- Promptly report issues arising in relation to students during the internship and practical process.

## 12. Implementation

- This document shall take effect from the date of signing and shall replace other relevant documents and regulations.
- In case of special circumstances arising during implementation, units shall report to the Rectorate Board of Vietnam-Japan University (via the Department of Training and Student Affairs) for consideration and decision./.
